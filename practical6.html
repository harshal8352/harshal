Tribute to Mahatma Gandhi

The Father of the Nation

About Mahatma Gandhi

Mahatma Gandhi, born on October 2, 1869, was a leader of the Indian independence movement against British rule. He is known for his philosophy of non-violence and civil disobedience, which inspired movements for civil rights and freedom across the world.

Key Principles

  • Ahimsa (Non-violence)
  • Satyagraha (Truth-force)
  • Self-reliance
  • Simple living

Legacy

Gandhi's teachings and philosophy continue to inspire millions around the globe. His commitment to peace and justice has made him a symbol of non-violent resistance and a champion of human rights.
